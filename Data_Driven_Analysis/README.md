# Data-Driven Analysis: U.S. EV Charging Infrastructure

A data analysis project examining the state of electric vehicle charging across the United States — built to move from raw public data to **actionable product and policy insights**, not just charts.

![Power BI dashboard and project summary](./ev-charging-powerbi-dashboard-and-summary.jpg)

*The asset above combines the Power BI dashboard with a written summary of objectives, key findings, and recommended actions.*

---

## Objective

Analyze U.S. EV charging infrastructure using data from the **U.S. Department of Energy's Alternative Fuels Data Center** to surface gaps in sustainability, accessibility, and ownership — and identify where investment and product decisions would have the highest impact on adoption.

---

## Tools

- **Power BI** — interactive dashboard for exploring station distribution, ownership, charger types, and energy sources
- **Public datasets** — U.S. DOE Alternative Fuels Data Center (EV charging station registry)

---

## Key Findings

### 1. Sustainability gap
Despite growth since 2010, only **0.5%** of charging stations run on renewable energy. Most infrastructure still depends on fossil-fuel-powered grids — undermining the environmental case for EV adoption at scale.

### 2. Fast-charging shortage
Only **16%** of stations offer **DC Fast Charging (Level 3)**. The majority rely on Level 2 chargers (6–7 hours for a full charge), creating a real barrier for long-distance and time-sensitive travel.

### 3. Private ownership concentration
~**85%** of stations are privately owned (Tesla, ChargePoint, Electrify America). While **94%** are publicly accessible, private control raises questions about equitable access, pricing, and long-term network reliability.

### 4. Geographic imbalance
Networks cluster in urban areas and leading states like **California**, leaving highways, small towns, and rural communities underserved — directly impacting range anxiety and adoption outside major metros.

## From Data to Action

The analysis doesn't stop at findings. It maps insights to concrete growth levers — the kind of product and systems thinking I apply in engineering work:

| Recommendation | Rationale |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Public investment in rural hubs** | Close the highway and small-town coverage gap where private operators have less incentive |
| **Nationwide EV warranty policies** | Extend proven state-level protections (e.g. California battery mandates) to build consumer confidence |
| **Renewable-powered station incentives** | Align infrastructure growth with actual emissions reduction goals |
| **Battery recycling policy** | Support second-life applications and responsible end-of-life disposal |
| **AI-driven expansion planning** | Use geospatial data, demand signals, and traffic patterns to place stations where they'll serve the most users |
